Keynote Speakers

Gordie Hogg - Opening Ceremony

Gordie Hogg is a well-known Canadian politician who has served in all three levels of government. He was elected to White Rock council in 1974 while going to university and he served there for 20 years , the last 10 as Mayor. He was then elected to the provincal legislature and served for 20 years and as a minister in various ministries followed by an election to the House of Commons where he was chosen to be the Pacific caucus Chairperson. Born in Victoria, British Columbia, he grew up in a family that was deeply committed to public service, and this passion for serving his community has stayed with him throughout his life. He completed his PhD at Simon Fraser University in 2015 and was appointed an Adjunct Professor. His dissertation was entitled "Creating Public Policy in a Complex Society: The Contexts, The Processes ,The Decisions". He had quickly gained a reputation as a hard-working and dedicated public servant, and was known for his commitment to protecting the rights and freedoms of all Canadians. In addition to his political career, he has served on over 20 non profit boards and committees.Throughout he has been committed to improving the lives of Canadians and he has worked tirelessly to address the many challenges faced by our citizens. Gordie's political career is marked by his commitment to public service, and he is widely respected for his integrity and dedication to the people he serves.

In recognition of his tireless work he has been recognized with many special honours and awards. He continues to sit on nonprofit boards and committees while focusing on reimaging our democracy as we face the new challenges of this century.

Brenda Locke - Closing Ceremony

Brenda Locke is a highly accomplished public servant who has dedicated her career to serving the people of Surrey, British Columbia. With over a decade of experience in public service, she has established a reputation as a knowledgeable, competent, and compassionate leader. Brenda was raised in Surrey and has a deep understanding of the city's unique challenges and opportunities. Throughout her career, she has demonstrated a commitment to addressing the needs of her constituents, particularly in the areas of public safety, job creation, and environmental sustainability. Her dedication and hard work paid off when she was elected as the new Mayor of Surrey in the recent election. With her vision for the city and her strong leadership skills, Brenda is poised to make a lasting impact on the lives of the people of Surrey. As Mayor, Brenda plans to prioritize the creation of new job opportunities, the improvement of public transportation options, and the promotion of clean energy initiatives. She is committed to building a city that is inclusive, equitable, and accessible to everyone.

Brenda's passion for public service and her commitment to improving the lives of the people of Surrey are qualities that set her apart as a leader. With her extensive experience and proven track record of success, she is well-equipped to lead Surrey into a bright and prosperous future.
